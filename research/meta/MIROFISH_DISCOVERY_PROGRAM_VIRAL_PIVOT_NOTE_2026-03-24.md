# MiroFish Discovery Program Viral Pivot Note

## Why

The next discovery phase should optimize for what people are actively interested in now: creator growth, gaming/community, agent builders, startups, productivity, careers, consumer agents, crypto, and X-native identity/status loops.

The prior pilot scaffold was structurally useful, but it leaned too far toward enterprise-adjacent wedges for the current growth thesis. Early growth is more likely to come from public builders, creators, crypto-native users, and X-native operators who will use, share, and talk about these domains in public.

## What Changed

- Replaced the default `100`-agent pilot cluster plan with a viral-first, X-native allocation.
- Added explicit X persona archetypes to the agent brief.
- Tightened the discovery brief so candidates should favor:
  - repeated use loops
  - creator / builder pull
  - talkability and social spread
  - domains people would use, compare, or brag about on X
- Generated a fresh `pilot_100_viral` scaffold, cluster bundle, brief, materialized directory, and zero-state progress snapshot without overwriting the earlier security pilot artifacts.

## New Default Cluster Plan

- `creator-growth-systems`
- `gaming-npc-community`
- `agentic-builders`
- `startup-founder-systems`
- `productivity-builder-ops`
- `career-status-social-proof`
- `consumer-agent-utilities`
- `crypto-defi-trading`
- `x-native-persona-tools`
- `design-remix-aesthetics`

## Immediate Next Step

Fill the `creator-growth-systems` cluster first, then move to `gaming-npc-community` and `agentic-builders`. Those three wedges best match the new growth thesis and are the right bridge to a later `250`-agent dress rehearsal.
