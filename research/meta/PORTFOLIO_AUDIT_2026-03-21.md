# Portfolio Audit: 2026-03-21

## Scope

Current Desktop domain-chip portfolio, audited from live discovery and a fresh portfolio-v3 run.

## Snapshot

- discovered chip-like directories: `20`
- valid/scored chips: `19`
- portfolio average: `41.7/100`
- verdict distribution: `8 beta`, `5 alpha`, `6 scaffold`
- one discovered directory is not a valid chip: `domain-chip-predictive-worlds-lab-spark-scaffold`

## Best Current Chips

- `domain-chip-startup-yc` at `72.0`: clear portfolio leader and the closest thing to a flagship
- `domain-chip-xcontent` at `65.6`: strong, but currently carries `single_day_batch`
- `domain-chip-pokemon-red` at `62.8`: very large artifact and vault depth
- `domain-chip-content` at `62.0`: strong doctrine and evidence shape
- `domain-chip-agentic-marketing` at `60.0`: live and substantial, but trajectory is not cleanly improving

## Findings

- The portfolio is top-heavy. The best chips look real, but more than half the scored portfolio is still `alpha` or `scaffold`.
- The growth cluster is the weakest part of the portfolio. `growth-analytics`, `growth-cgo`, `growth-distribution`, and `gstack` are mostly structure without operating history and are flagged `high_structure_low_velocity`.
- `xcontent` is promising but should not be overtrusted yet because of the `single_day_batch` anti-gaming flag.
- Several beta chips are broader than they are grounded. `web-designer`, `spark-private-main`, and `predictive-worlds-lab` still have shallow contradiction rigor or no watchtower depth.
- The inventory itself has drift: one discovered directory is not a valid chip and should not be treated as active portfolio surface.

## Good

- There is a real leadership tier now, not just one standout chip.
- Most live chips share the same four-hook surface and manifest contract.
- A few chips have genuinely deep artifact and vault volume, especially `startup-yc`, `pokemon-red`, and `spark-private`.

## Bad

- Average portfolio quality is still low for something that wants to claim a mature chip ecosystem.
- Too many chips exist as scaffolds or partial shells instead of focused, compounding products.
- The portfolio narrative is inflated if the growth cluster is counted at the same weight as the real operating chips.

## Ugly

- `growth-analytics`, `growth-cgo`, and `growth-distribution` have `0` artifacts and `0` vault docs while still appearing as active chips.
- `domain-chip-predictive-worlds-lab-spark-scaffold` is discoverable by name but is not a valid chip.

## What I Would Do Next

1. Treat `startup-yc`, `xcontent`, `pokemon-red`, `content`, `agentic-marketing`, and `spark-private` as the active leadership set.
2. Pick at most two growth chips to mature; archive or quarantine the rest from portfolio-level claims until they have real runs and evidence.
3. Run an anti-gaming review on `xcontent`.
4. Clean the inventory narrative so invalid or shell directories stop counting as ecosystem proof.
