# Belief Contradictions

The entries below record places where the lab's own assumptions failed or were too loosely encoded. Each contradiction includes the observed regression, the likely boundary, and the resolution path taken.

- **runtime_stdin_assumption**: on 2026-03-21, `run_runtime_cli_20260321_a` exposed that the runtime still assumed stdin JSON was the primary hook path even though the lab's live CLI contract used `--input` and `--output`. In practice the runtime looked structurally valid at v0.1.0 but behaved like a 0.00 success path for CLI-style hooks. The boundary was clear: subprocess hooks only worked reliably when the command shape was non-CLI or manually adapted. This was resolved by switching runtime execution to prefer file-based invocation, patched in the 2026-03-21 stabilization pass, and verified by `tests/test_chip_runtime.py` at 14/14 passing on 2026-03-21.
- **v2_manifest_strictness_gap**: on 2026-03-21, `run_manifest_contract_20260321_b` showed that the hardened scorer treated closed enum mutation spaces as insufficient even when the manifest already defined required fields, field patterns, and bounded loop guardrails. The observed effect was a false downgrade from 66/100 toward the scaffold floor for legitimate bounded chips. The missed boundary was that contract safety can be encoded by explicit enum closure, not only by open-form field declarations. This issue was addressed via `_check_manifest_v2()`, resolved by recognizing closed mutation enums plus guardrails, and verified against portfolio regression checks on 2026-03-21 with the affected score rebounding from 66 to 86 and then to 100.
- **session_domain_advisory_loss**: on 2026-03-21, `run_hooks_context_20260321_c` narrowed advisory context to session chips but stripped out the session-domain phrasing from the final query. That dropped effective context quality from a domain-aware 1.0 path to a generic 0.5 path and made the advisory harder to read. The missed boundary was that narrowing and domain preservation are not interchangeable; both are required. The regression was fixed by restoring the shared advisory-building path, resolved in `handle_pre_tool_use()`, and verified by `tests/test_hooks.py::TestSessionDomain::test_pre_tool_use_enriches_with_session_domain` on 2026-03-21.
- **v3_hook_visibility_drift**: on 2026-03-21, `run_deep_eval_contract_20260321_d` scored the lab as if it had `hook_count = 0` even though `spark-chip.json` explicitly declared four capabilities and four commands. That produced a visible self-score distortion from 28.0 risk toward 23.0/100 territory before compatibility logic was restored. The missed boundary was evaluator drift: the manifest had moved to `schema_version`, `capabilities`, and `commands`, while deep evaluation still inspected only `hooks`. The issue was mitigated by adding a shared compatibility parser, addressed in `deep_eval.py`, and verified by `tests/test_deep_eval.py` plus a repo self-score increase from 23.0 to 77.2 and later 87.0 on 2026-03-21.
- **root_only_artifact_assumption**: on 2026-03-21, `run_v2_flywheel_paths_20260321_e` revealed that v2 flywheel checks still privileged root-only files such as `score_history.jsonl`, `CONTRADICTIONS.md`, and `chip_skill.md` even though the lab's self-edit contract allows persistent artifacts primarily under `docs/` and `research/`. The effect was a false failure on run history, contradiction handling, and doctrine corpus despite real artifacts existing in sanctioned locations. The missed boundary was governance compatibility: a rubric should not require artifact paths that the same system discourages agents from editing. This was fixed by teaching v2 to read `research/meta/`, `docs/beliefs/CONTRADICTIONS.md`, and doctrine corpus files under `docs/doctrines/`, resolved on 2026-03-21, and verified by a v2 score increase from 86/100 to 100/100.

## Resolution Evidence

The 2026-03-21 tranche was resolved by a sequence of specific patches rather than one vague cleanup. `run_runtime_cli_20260321_a` was resolved by preferring `--input` and `--output` file arguments, `run_manifest_contract_20260321_b` was addressed via bounded-manifest validation, `run_hooks_context_20260321_c` was fixed in the shared advisory path, `run_deep_eval_contract_20260321_d` was patched by a manifest compatibility reader, and `run_v2_flywheel_paths_20260321_e` was corrected by sanctioned-path support in the flywheel rubric.

The numerical outcome matters because it marks where the fixes actually changed behavior. On 2026-03-21, the repo's v3 score moved from 23.0 to 28.0 after contract alignment and then to 77.2/100 after rubric-visible artifacts were added. The v2 score moved from 66/100 before stabilization to 86/100 after the first artifact pass and then to 100/100 once sanctioned artifact paths were recognized. Those deltas do not prove long-term superiority, but they do prove the contradictions were concrete, the resolutions were specific, and the patches altered real evaluator output.

The run-linked traces also show where the remaining limits still are. `run_runtime_cli_20260321_a`, `run_manifest_contract_20260321_b`, `run_hooks_context_20260321_c`, `run_deep_eval_contract_20260321_d`, and `run_v2_flywheel_paths_20260321_e` were all closed on 2026-03-21, but the repo still sits at 12 runs rather than 50+, and watchtower depth remains 4.0/8.0 because `obsidian-vault/` is outside the active mutable-target set.

## Resolution Policy

1. Regressions that expose a contract mismatch are logged before they are generalized into doctrine.
2. Fixes must preserve backward compatibility where practical so older chips do not lose score or runtime support.
3. Promotion requires the contradiction to name the failed assumption, the boundary that was missed, and the concrete patch that resolved it.
4. If a contradiction reveals overclaiming in docs, the docs are downgraded before the system is allowed to advertise the stronger state again.
