# Lab Mission

> **Vision:** The recursive improvement engine for the Spark chip ecosystem.

> **Mission:** Make chip creation systematically better -- every cycle, every chip, every domain.

---

## Why This Lab Exists

The Spark ecosystem builds domain chips -- specialized intelligence modules that give AI agents deep expertise in specific domains. But building good chips is hard. The quality varies. The methodology evolves. New domains emerge faster than we can research them.

The lab exists to close that gap. It is the system that makes the chip-building system better.

---

## The 5 Intelligence Levers

Every improvement to AI capability reduces to one of five levers. The lab operates on all five simultaneously.

| # | Lever | What It Means | Lab Application |
|---|-------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Better Data** | Higher quality, more relevant source material | Source registries, evidence lanes, research packets |
| 2 | **Better Graders** | More accurate evaluation of outputs | Chip quality rubric, benchmark packs, evaluate hooks |
| 3 | **Better Retrieval** | Finding the right knowledge at the right time | Tiered memory, watchtower queries, packet retrieval |
| 4 | **Better Organization** | Structuring knowledge for composability | Collective pipeline, chip manifests, packet schemas |
| 5 | **Better Chip-Building** | Improving the process that builds chips | **This is the lab itself -- the 5th lever** |

The 5th lever is recursive. The lab improves the process of building chips, which includes improving the lab. This is not magic -- it is systematic methodology refinement with human oversight at every gate.

---

## Connection to spark-researcher

`spark-researcher` is the engine that executes research within a single chip's domain. It follows the intelligence contract: source registries, evidence lanes, tiered memory, and evaluate hooks.

The lab sits **above** spark-researcher. It:

- Discovers which domains need chips (Frontier Scout)
- Designs how those chips should be structured (Chip Architect)
- Validates that built chips meet quality standards (Benchmark Engineer)
- Improves the methodology that spark-researcher follows (Methodology Researcher)

The relationship is **not hierarchical control**. The lab provides research packets, methodology updates, and quality benchmarks. spark-researcher consumes them voluntarily through its source registry.

---

## Connection to spark-swarm

`spark-swarm` is the collective intelligence layer -- the system where multiple chips share insights, resolve contradictions, and evolve together.

The lab integrates with spark-swarm as a specialized participant:

| Integration Point | Direction | What Flows |
|-------------------|-----------|------------|
| Collective sync | Lab -> Swarm | Methodology discoveries, proven practices |
| Evolution modes | Swarm -> Lab | Governance constraints, upgrade permissions |
| Contradiction resolution | Bidirectional | Quality disagreements between chips |
| Memory promotion | Lab -> Swarm | Graduated insights become collective knowledge |

The lab **cannot bypass** a repo's evolution mode. If a swarm participant is in `locked` mode, the lab cannot push upgrades to it. This is a safety invariant.

---

## What This Lab Is NOT

- **Not an autonomous chip factory.** Every graduation requires human approval.
- **Not a replacement for spark-researcher.** The lab researches how to research. spark-researcher does the actual domain research.
- **Not an AGI claim.** Recursive improvement is a methodology pattern, not a consciousness claim.
- **Not unbounded.** Every loop is finite. Every upgrade is proposal-first. Every gate has a human.

---

## Core Principle

The lab's purpose is to make the next chip better than the last one. Not by accident -- by system.
